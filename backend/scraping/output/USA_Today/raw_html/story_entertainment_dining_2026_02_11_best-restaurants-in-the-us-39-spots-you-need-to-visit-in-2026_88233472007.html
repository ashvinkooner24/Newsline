Restaurants of the Year celebrates standout dining across US for 2026
USA TODAY’s Restaurants of the Year spotlights top dining destinations across America for 2026, from roadside gems to chef-driven hotspots. See the list Feb. 11.

Our USA TODAY Network food writers love sharing their picks for the top local spots, from best steakhouses to favorite dive bars.

But none of those lists compares to our popular Restaurants of the Year, which debuted in 2024.

Looking for a memorable meal — whether close to home or on your next trip? USA TODAY’s 2026 Restaurants of the Year list highlights standout dining destinations across the country, from humble roadside gems to chef-driven hotspots earning national buzz.

Chosen by USA TODAY Network food journalists who know their local scenes best, the list celebrates places that offer not just great flavors but also heart, hospitality and a strong sense of community.

You’ll find deftly prepared barbecue, fresh seafood, handmade pastas, globally inspired plates and more. These are the spots our reporters return to, rave about and recommend to friends — and now, to you.

Huarachis | Phoenix, Arizona

Details: 814 N. Central Ave.; 602-773-1413, huarachis.com

Huarachis restaurant review: Our food writer says, whatever you do, you can't skip the papas al disco.

Huarachis Taqueria named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Huarachis Taqueria in Phoenix/Arizona, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

Huarachis is the first solo venture for Rene Andrade, the 2024 James Beard winner for Best Chef Southwest. Inside the pink-hued restaurant, guests bob in their seats to a variety of musical genres – including cumbia, corridos, merengue, salsa, rap and R&B – while sipping on agave cocktails and perusing a menu of tacos, tortas, vampiros and sides like the killer papas al disco made with baja crema, olive oil, chiltepin peppers and queso cotija. During the week, guests can order tortas mamalonas. The carnitas torta is a savory blast of toasted bread, crispy-edge cheese and charred meat stacked with creamy avocado and soft ham slices. Sharp onions and fresh tomatoes cut through the fat. The restaurant is playful, the dishes and drinks solid and the chef is known as a mentor and a consistent force for good in the community. There’s nothing not to love. — Bahar Anooshahr, Arizona Republic

Indibar | Scottsdale, Arizona

Details: 6208 N. Scottsdale Road; 480-219-9774, theindibar.com

Indibar restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the non-vegetarian sampler and the chef's signature lamb chops.

Indibar named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Indibar in Scottsdale/Arizona, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026

Indibar was born from Dr. Jonathan Rodrigues’ love of Indian food and mixology. Originally from an area near Goa, India, he wanted to create a place to honor the act of sharing a meal in a modern way. He has done just that with a restaurant that features opulent pops of gold but doesn't require a dress code. This balance of fine dining and homestyle hospitality continues with familiar dishes presented in modern and elegant ways. Some of the best are the chef’s signature lamb chop and kheema dosa. The bar program includes cocktails with personal backstories and spice-box flavors, including nonalcoholic sips like the delicate, floral Honey Heist, made with Lyre’s Dry London spirit, cardamom, saffron, honey and ginger. It’s the kind of restaurant where there is always a new bouquet of flavors to try. — Bahar Anooshahr, Arizona Republic

The Stand | Salt River Pima-Maricopa Indian Community near Scottsdale, Arizona

Details: 3996 N. Alma School Road; 480-519-1108, facebook.com/SRTheStand

The Stand restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the frybread topped with green chili.

The Stand named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a peek at The Stand on Salt River Pima Maricopa Indian Reservation near Scottsdale, Arizona, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

Never has a restaurant’s name been so fitting as The Stand – a literal, wooden lean-to on the Salt River Pima-Maricopa Indian Community, complete with handwritten menu signs and a ceiling made of tarps. But don’t let the humble exterior fool you. The Stand offers one of the truest tastes of a dish that embodies the culture of Arizona and the Southwest: frybread. The menu includes frybread topped with red or green chili, beans and cheese or ground meat. The restaurant also makes Native-style tacos. Owner Mick Washington makes the frybread and chumuth (Indigenous-style tortillas) by hand. There isn’t a lot of seating. And you’ll have to park in a dirt lot. But the fluffy, fresh frybread and meltingly tender, meaty green chili are worth getting your shoes a little dirty for. — Eddie Fontanez, Arizona Republic

El Mexicali Café II | Indio, California

Details: 43-430 Monroe St., Suite A; 760-342-2333, elmexicalicafe2.com

El Mexicali Café II restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the Especial Del Mexicali — grilled skirt steak with sauteed cactus — and the famous shrimp-stuffed yellow pepper.

El Mexicali Cafe II named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside El Mexicali Cafe II in Indio, California, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

In a region with no shortage of great Mexican restaurants, El Mexicali Café II has legions of devotees drawn to its friendly, familial atmosphere, menu heavy on classic, time-tested dishes and, perhaps most of all, the famous Chiles Gueritos Rellenos de Camarón. That's the beloved appetizer consisting of six yellow peppers stuffed with shrimp that family matriarch Carmen Murguia brought to California from the border town of Mexicali, creating what may be the area’s most popular Mexican dish and inspiring countless imitators. A more spacious and vibrantly decorated alternative to the original restaurant in downtown Indio – both have the same menu – El Mexicali II’s spot near Interstate 10 makes it a popular stop for those traveling between Southern California and Arizona. — Paul Albani-Burgio, Palm Springs Desert Sun

Postcard Pizza | Fort Collins, Colorado

Details: 133 Remington St.; 205-920-2988, postcard.pizza

Postcard Pizza restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the global specials at this Detroit-style pizza joint.

Postcard Pizza named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Postcard Pizza in Fort Collins, Colorado, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

Tucked inside Fort Collins' Equinox Brewing, Postcard Pizza has pushed what the city knows about pizza to a new crispy, cheesy edge. The two-man operation dishes up Detroit-style pizza out of its cozy brewery shop and has become known for its inventive weekly specialty pies. Try its specials, which have included a banh mi pie and a potato pesto variety, or go with its trusty favorites, such as the classic fancy pepperoni with cupping pepperoni, swirls of whipped ricotta and drizzles of hot honey. Don't forget to pair it with a beer from Equinox Brewery, which lends its lived-in Fort Collins brewery vibes to Postcard Pizza's new, but growing, operation. — Abigail Flores-Johnson, Fort Collins Coloradoan

Lewes Oyster House | Lewes, Delaware 

Details: 108 Second St.; 302-644-7258, lewesoyster.com

Lewes Oyster House restaurant review: Our food writer loves the fresh oysters — a dozen varieties daily — and the sophisticated vibe of the downtown historic building.

Lewes Oyster House named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside the Lewes Oyster House in Lewes, Delaware, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

From its raw, charbroiled or fried oysters to its Old Bay and malt vinegar boardwalk fries, the Lewes Oyster House is a love letter to Delaware beach memories. Walls are decorated with antique oyster plates at this "tavern by the sea" in a historic downtown building. Rest elbows at the bar, get a margarita and snack on crispy coconut shrimp served with chili garlic sauce. Seafood is the star here, with dishes such as the buttermilk-cornmeal-dredged fried Chesapeake Bay catfish, but the Damn Good Burger and the turkey burger should not be ignored. The restaurant's new Gallo Room is a go-to spot for craft cocktails, bold small plates and a welcoming vibe. — Patricia Talorico, Delaware News Journal

Tide Tables | Bradenton, Florida

Details: 12507 Cortez Road West; 941-567-6206, tidetablescortez.com

Tide Tables restaurant review: Our food writer says sea scallops are a sleeper hit, and the Key lime pie is worth saving room for — especially at sunset.

Sarasota-area restaurant named one of the best in the U.S. See video
Take a look inside Tide Tables in Bradenton, Florida, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

If you appreciate impeccably fresh grouper in a flip-flops-friendly setting – perhaps paired with an ice-cold beer – chart your course for Tide Tables. The waterfront favorite on Florida’s gorgeous Gulf Coast cuts its fish right on the dock along the Intracoastal Waterway, with views toward Anna Maria Island in the historic commercial fishing village of Cortez. That just-caught seafood shines in dishes like a perfectly blackened grouper sandwich, rotating specials such as Thai chili grouper bites, and crowd-pleasing fish tacos topped with sesame ginger sauce. Sea scallops are a sleeper hit, and the Key lime pie is worth saving room for – especially at sunset. — Wade Tatangelo, Sarasota Herald-Tribune

Oceano | Lake Worth Beach, Florida

Details: 512 Lucerne Ave.; 561-400-7418, oceanolwb.com

Oceano restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the ever-changing seasonal menu, free of gimmicks or over-the-top presentations — with honest, exciting food rooted in skill and integrity.

Oceano named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Oceano in Lake Worth Beach, Florida, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

Eclectic new American Oceano is the kind of restaurant that quietly sets the tone. Jeremy and Cindy Bearman, a husband-wife duo with pedigrees from New York City’s ABC Kitchen and db Bistro Moderne, have created a seasonal menu that is technique-forward and expressive. Jeremy’s savory dishes are composed and confident; standouts have included a vibrant duck salad, delicate waloo crudo, mussels escabeche and expertly prepared American red snapper. Cindy’s desserts are vivid, layered and unapologetically indulgent. Oceano’s influence is palpable: When chefs talk about where they actually eat, it’s always part of the conversation. The Bearmans don’t chase buzz, instead they define it, one plate at a time. — Diana Biederman, Palm Beach Post

Bluebeard | Indianapolis, Indiana

Details: 653 Virginia Ave.; 317-686-1580, bluebeardindy.com

Bluebeard restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the inventive pasta dishes and seasonal salads at this cozy spot named for a Kurt Vonnegut novel.

Bluebeard named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Bluebeard in Indianapolis, Indiana, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

Guests crunch through colorful seasonal salads, sip from an expansive wine list and dig into chef Alan Sternberg's inventive pasta dishes at snugly cloistered tables in wood and brick alcoves lined with paintings, books and even a handful of typewriters. Overlooking the Cultural Trail in Indianapolis' Fletcher Place neighborhood, Bluebeard has drawn curious eaters since it opened in 2012 under the father-son duo of Tom and Ed Battista, and now it has reached another, different summit.  Like the 1987 Kurt Vonnegut novel from which the restaurant took its name, Bluebeard encourages guests to drop their guard, surround themselves with other people and try something that frightens them a little.  — Bradley Hohulin, Indianapolis Star

Prime & Providence | West Des Moines, Iowa

Details: 595 S. 60th St.; 515-644-6805, primeandprovidence.com

Prime & Providence restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the show-stopping Caesar salad and the dishes cooked on the custom-designed 15-foot hearth using Fogo Charcoal.

Prime & Providence named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Prime & Providence in West Des Moines, Iowa, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

Prime & Providence, a modern, upscale steakhouse and oyster bar that blends sophistication with approachability, feels like a restaurant that should be in New York, Chicago or Dallas. Opened in May 2024 by chef and co-owner Dominic Iannarelli, the restaurant is built around live-fire cooking on a custom-designed 15-foot hearth using Fogo Charcoal, which gives dishes such as show-stopping Caesar salad, Wagyu steaks and rolled lasagnas a distinct smoky depth. The ambiance is warm and elegant, featuring statement lighting, a spectacular wine wall, and liquor lockers for purchase. — Susan Stapleton, Des Moines Register

Whatcha Smokin? BBQ + Brew | Luther, Iowa

Details: 403 Iowa Ave.; 515-257-7490, whatchasmokinbbq.com

Whatcha Smokin? restaurant review: Our food writer recommends lining up early to taste the fall-apart-tender brisket at this Central Texas-style joint.

Whatcha Smokin? named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Whatcha Smokin? BBQ + Brew in Luther, Iowa, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

Whatcha Smokin? in little Luther, Iowa, a blip on the map in the middle of nowhere, serves some of the best barbecue you’ve ever eaten. People come from all over Iowa to wait in line for Central Texas-style fatty brisket so tender it melts in your mouth, burnt ends cubed for weekly specials, and pulled pork that’s dry-rubbed with five spices and smoked for 12-14 hours for deep flavor and bark. All are smoked in-house with red oak and white oak wood in a Moberg smoker. — Susan Stapleton, Des Moines Register

Volare | Louisville, Kentucky

Details: 2300 Frankfort Ave.; 502-894-4446, volare-restaurant.com

Volare restaurant review: Our food writer says not to miss the Rotollini Di Melanzane or the Tiramisu, a classic decadent Italian treat that features ladyfingers soaked in espresso and layered with a sweet mascarpone Frangelico cream.

Volare Italian Ristorante named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Volare Italian Ristorante in Louisville, Kentucky, named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants for 2026.

Across from shops fixing cars and selling coffee, a black-and-white brick building is where locals go for a place that’s classic and classy, though scrumptious bar bites and a half-price wine night are everyday draws. Regarded as a Louisville treasure since 2004, Volare is synonymous with special occasions, house-made pastas, desserts with decorative swirls, and ingredients grown on a 10-acre Kentucky farm owned by executive and head pastry chef Josh Moore. Named for the Italian phrase “to fly,” the restaurant continues to take traditional dishes to new heights. Patrons swear by its braised short ribs, roasted duck, and seafood linguine. — Amanda Hancock, Louisville Courier Journal

Lea's Lunchroom | Lecompte, Louisiana

Details: 1810 Highway 71 South; 318-776-5178, leaslunchroom.com

Lea's Lunchroom restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the pie — Lea's is the reason Lecompte is known as the Pie Capital of Louisiana.

Lea's Lunchroom named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Lea's Lunchroom in Lecompte, Louisiana, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

Enjoy a slice of Americana at this classic diner, where you'll be taken back to another time as you dine to music by singers like Elvis Presley and Louisiana's own Jerry Lee Lewis. Lea's Lunchroom is a family-owned business founded in 1928 by Lea Johnson, Lea's is the reason Lecompte is known as the Pie Capital of Louisiana. You'll find flavors such as apple, pecan, cherry, lemon and chocolate meringue, and coconut. Lea's serves breakfast and lunch – but don’t expect to look at a menu. You'll hear what they have for breakfast from the waitress. What's being served for lunch each day is written on a chalkboard in the dining room – all staples of good Southern cooking. — Melinda Martinez, Alexandria Town Talk 

Sarma | Somerville, Massachusetts

Details: 249 Pearl St.; 617-764-4464, sarmarestaurant.com

Sarma restaurant review: Our food writer says you can't miss the dukkah fried dates and that the cocktails are a must.

Sarma named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Sarma in Somerville, Massachusetts, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

Step inside Sarma, owned by talented eight-time James Beard nominee Cassie Piuma, through its embroidered drapes and feel transported from Somerville’s Winter Hill neighborhood to the best-kept secret in town. You'll be greeted by colorful hanging mosaic globe lanterns and a wall covered with hand painted ceramic plates. The energy and excitement of a diverse crowd buzzes as silverware and glasses clink. Come hungry, because you’ll want to try many shareable dishes that will amaze you with their savory, adventurous flavor combinations and creative presentation, like dukkah fried dates (carrot matbucha, goat cheese, jalapeno honey). Cocktails/mocktails and dessert are musts. You’ll leave full, but happy. — Joanna Tzouvelis, Wicked Local

Spencer | Ann Arbor, Michigan

Details: 113 E. Liberty; 734-369-3979, spencerannarbor.com

Spencer restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the seasonal menu, such as pole beans and stone fruit on a summer menu, and a winter squash risotto in the winter months.

Spencer named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Spencer in Ann Arbor, Michigan, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

By day, Spencer is a wine shop, with rows of hundreds of natural wines. By night, it moonlights as a cozy fine dining restaurant centered on seasonal ingredients. The room is transformed with sturdy, wooden communal tables, where diners rub elbows as they cut into fresh pastas and tear apart pieces of hot, fluffy bread. The tasting menu here is dictated by produce availability at nearby farms and preserves from previous peak seasons, like pickled cherries in December. When the temperature is right, enjoy lunch at the patio bar, where the best of the restaurant and the wine shop converge for a standout meal paired with quality libations. Ten years in, Spencer offers something magical in the heart of Ann Arbor any time of day. — Lyndsay C. Green, Detroit Free Press

Mayflower Cafe | Jackson, Mississippi

Details: 123 W. Capitol St.; 601-355-4122, themayflowercafe.com

Mayflower Cafe restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the fresh seafood, which comes directly from the display case with the catch of the day.

Mayflower Cafe named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Mayflower Cafe in Jackson, Mississippi, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

Mayflower Cafe, originally opened in 1935, is a piece of Jackson history recently brought back to life by owners Cody McCain and Hunter Evans, a renowned local chef. In 2024, the pair restored the restaurant using old photos and newspaper articles as references. The 1950's-style exterior has made appearances in film, including "The Help." Changing hands multiple times in its 90-year history, the Mayflower has served dishes from hamburgers to chop suey. The restaurant eventually settled into a menu of fresh seafood, with locals dubbing the Mayflower "Mississippi's Original Seafood House." Today, Evans serves up many of the same time-honored dishes, and the once-abandoned seafood display case in the front window stays stocked with the catch of the day. — Mary Boyte, Mississippi Clarion Ledger

Esther's Kitchen | Las Vegas, Nevada

Details: 1131 S. Main St.; 702-570-7864, estherslv.com

Esther's Kitchen restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the housemade sourdough and pasta alla chitarra.

Esther's Kitchen named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Esther's Kitchen in Las Vegas, Nevada, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

The Las Vegas Strip is dotted with five-star restaurants surrounded with dress-to-get-noticed tourists awaiting their moment for once-in-a-lifetime dining. But just a few miles north, it’s Las Vegas locals who are circling for a parking spot in the city’s Arts District, hoping to make their reservation time at Esther’s Kitchen. The environment is comfortable. The dress code is casual. The servers are over-the-top attentive. The menu is elevated, but without pretension. The sourdough bread will make you rethink how good meals should begin. The dessert wine list will make you wonder how your visit passed so quickly. And the rotating menu of seasonal entrees makes every seating – brunch, lunch and dinner – feel like the restaurant’s main event. — Brett McGinness, Reno Gazette Journal

The Pasta Shop | Denville, New Jersey

Details: 13 First Ave.; 973-253-4143, thepastashopdenville.com

The Pasta Shop restaurant review: Our food writer says the sometimes three-hour wait is worth it, especially for the sweet corn and mascarpone ravioli, dripping in truffle butter.

The Pasta Shop named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside The Pasta Shop in Denville, New Jersey, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

A three hour wait rarely deters customers from lining up for a seat at The Pasta Shop, a “neighborhood Italian eatery” in Denville, New Jersey. On a nightly basis - and also Saturdays for lunch – Tommy Pollio’s suburban gem turns out hand-rolled pasta and wood-fired entrées smothered in à la minute sauces, high-end cheeses and more. Order the Sweet Corn and Mascarpone Ravioli, for example, and be wowed by mascarpone-stuffed pillows dripping in truffle butter and basil. Go for the Chicken Parm, on another note, and be blown away by an award-winning cutlet with a side of savory garlic linguine. — Kara VanDooijeweert, NorthJersey.com

Ram & Rooster | Metuchen, New Jersey

Details: 83 Central Ave.; 732-662-1041, ramandroosternj.com

Ram & Rooster restaurant review: Our food writer thinks the upscale, seasonal food — like lotus root croquettes — makes this elegant Chinese restaurant worth the trip.

Ram & Rooster named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Ram & Rooster in Metuchen, New Jersey, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

Sean Yan opened Ram & Rooster − the only Chinese prix fixe restaurant in New Jersey − in 2025 as a love letter to his Chinese heritage. But through Yan's Western takes on Chinese flavors, he has created a haute destination named after his parents' zodiac signs. Hailing from Heirloom Kitchen, a 2024 USA TODAY Restaurant of the Year, Yan only offers a prix fixe menu, with dishes such as lotus root croquette, black soybean pudding, and honey shrimp toast. Fare is served in an intimate space warmed with walnut, pendant lighting and notes of gold in an Asian-style building. Just 28 seats, including an eight-seat chef's counter, fill two floors. — Jenna Intersimone, MyCentralJersey.com

Anjelica's Restaurant | Sea Bright, New Jersey

Details: 1070 Ocean Ave.; 732-842-2800, anjelicas.com

Anjelica's restaurant review: Our food writer says the from-scratch pasta (including a secret, off-menu spicy rigatoni) and the charming twinkle-lit alley make a month-long wait for reservations totally worth it.

Anjelica's Restaurant named a USA TODAY best restaurant of 2026
Take a look inside Anjelica's Restaurant in Sea Bright, New Jersey, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026

Picture sitting down to Sunday dinner, only with white tablecloths and stellar service. This is Anjelica's, a warm, inviting restaurant serving traditional Italian and Italian-American dishes. The late Ray Lena opened Anjelica's with his family nearly three decades ago; today, his daughter – its namesake – heads a restaurant whose reservations fill up more than a month in advance. Try her favorite dish, linguine with garlic and olive oil, or ravioli made with scratch-made pasta and ricotta cheese. Diners in the know order the spicy rigatoni, which isn't on the menu but is a favorite dish, and request a table in the restaurant's charming, twinkle light-lit alley. — Sarah Griesemer, Asbury Park Press

Pizzeria La Rosa | New Rochelle, New York

Details: 12 Russell Ave.; 914-633-0800, pizzerialarosa.com

Pizzeria La Rosa restaurant review: Our food writer loves the nostalgic vibe of this cozy neighborhood spot, where you can indulge in terrific pizza, chicken parm and meatballs — and, on Thursdays, smashburgers.

Pizzeria La Rosa named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Pizzeria La Rosa in New Rochelle, New York, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

Some say it's the 100-year-old pizza oven that makes Pizzeria La Rosa special. But it is chef-owner Matt Di Gesu, who for years worked at Brooklyn's Best Pizza and has a masterful way with food. Though pizza is the draw, his hidden spot off a small side street offers more than pies. He's also known for his Thursday smashburgers, his chicken parm, meatballs, and roasted veggies. Di Gesu’s food, coupled with the restaurant's nostalgic vibe – a hodgepodge of records plus ‘70s and ‘80s photos of the city – make the pizzeria a cozy neighborhood spot. Adding to the charm: the medley of personal pics, including those of Vita La Rosa, Di Gesu’s Sicilian-born grandmother who taught him how to cook. The restaurant is named after her. — Jeanne Muchnick, Rockland/Westchester Journal News

Swan Market | Rochester, New York

Details: 231 Parsells Ave.; 585-288-5320, swanmarket.com

Swan Market restaurant review: Our food writer says to try the sampler platter at this German meat market-turned-restaurant, where an accordion player entertains on the last Friday of the month.

Swan Market named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Swan Market in Rochester, New York, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

This cozy building in a modest residential area has been home to a German meat market since the 1920s. Now it’s a popular place where people gather at communal tables to enjoy hearty helpings of Oktoberfest-type fare year round. Barry and Liz Fischer bought the market in 2000. When sales at the counter weren’t enough to sustain their young family, they put out picnic tables and started serving hot lunches. It now serves lunch Wednesday through Saturday; an accordion player entertains on the last Friday of each month. The most popular dish is the sampler platter, which gives you a taste of a variety of dishes: pork roast, roladen, currywurst, goulash over spaetzle, the schnitzel of the week, potato salad, red cabbage and sauerkraut. Enjoy it with a stein of German beer. Before you leave, be sure to pay a visit to the deli counter and take home some house-smoked bacon or one of their three kinds of liverwurst. — Tracy Schuhmacher, Rochester Democrat and Chronicle

Bear's Smokehouse BBQ | Asheville, North Carolina

Details: 135 Coxe Ave.; 828-505-7088, bearsbbq.com/asheville

Bear's Smokehouse BBQ restaurant review: Our food writer admires the all-out effort the restaurant gave to its community after Tropical Storm Helene, and says the Bear's Balls — pulled pork, mac-and-cheese, and cornbread, battered and deep-fried — are a don't miss.

Bear's Smokehouse BBQ named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026: Video
Take a look inside Bear's Smokehouse BBQ in Asheville, North Carolina, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

The slow-cooked meats and stuffed Bear Balls – pulled pork, mac-and-cheese and cornbread, all battered and deep-fried – make Bear’s Smokehouse BBQ worth the trip to downtown Asheville’s vibrant South Slope neighborhood. Enjoy blissfully chowing down in the dining room or hanging out in the adjacent bar and dog-friendly patio — or read up on the help-others philosophy of the restaurant and feel proud to support them. When Tropical Storm Helene devastated Western North Carolina in fall 2024, Bear’s, in collaboration with other local restaurants, became a hub for food preparation and distribution operations for the global food relief organization World Central Kitchen. More than 1 million meals were distributed across the region in the months following the historic storm. Bear’s also provides outreach through Linked4Life, in partnership with All Souls Counseling, to provide free therapy sessions for Asheville food and beverage workers. — Tiana Kennell, Asheville Citizen Times

Luminosa | Asheville, North Carolina

Details: 20 Battery Park Ave., Floor 1; 828-271-9808, luminosaavl.com

Luminosa restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the lemon pizza and bone marrow tater tots, but says to be open to surprises on this ever-changing menu.

Luminosa named one of USA TODAY’s best restaurants of 2026: Video
Take a look inside Luminosa in Asheville, North Carolina, named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants for 2026.

Luminosa, set in the ground floor of a historic landmark reimagined as a boutique hotel, merges Italian-style dishes with Appalachian ingredients, helping to set the Asheville restaurant apart in its brief time on the dining scene. Executive chef Graham House and chef de cuisine Sean McMullen honor global and regional cuisine and history through modern approaches and inventive recipes, using a wood-burning oven and grill. The lemon pizza and bone marrow tater tots wow diners, yet the regularly rotating menu centered around local harvests keeps guests guessing about what’s coming to their tables next. — Tiana Kennell, Asheville Citizen Times

Manna | Wilmington, North Carolina

Details: 123 Princess St.; 910-763-5252, mannaavenue.com

manna restaurant review: Our food writer recommends the twice monthly omakase dinner.

manna named one of USA TODAY's best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside manna in Wilmington, North Carolina, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

Located in a historic building in downtown Wilmington, this restaurant has been a local culinary destination for 15 years. It’s fine dining, but not stuffy. Chef Carson Jewell is a Wilmington native with impressive experience who offers his take on elevated Southern fare. The menu changes daily based on what’s available from a dozen or so farmers and purveyors. And the twice monthly omakase experience is a favorite with adventurous diners and always sells out. Owner Billy Mellon also takes service and his bar program seriously. A basement cellar is filled with around 4,000 bottles of 700 different wines. Sister businesses include a music/event venue next door, a private speakeasy and a dive bar. — Allison Ballard, Wilmington StarNews

Mita's | Cincinnati, Ohio

Details: 501 Race St.; 513-421-6482, mitas.co

Mita's restaurant review: Our food writer says start with the empanadas, then move on to the poached rock shrimp, pupusas and the pork chop, which come from acorn-fed black Iberian pigs raised in Spain on free-range farms.

Mita's named one of USA Today's best restaurants of 2026
Take a look inside Mita's in Cincinnati, Ohio, named one of USA TODAYs best restaurants for 2026.

The best restaurants have a story to tell. And Mita’s is no exception. Owner Jose Salazar's empanadas are inspired by the ones his Colombian grandmother, or mita, made when he was growing up, though he elevates the fillings with tender beef short ribs.

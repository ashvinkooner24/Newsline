I’m a travel editor — these are the 14 luggage brands I always recommend

By
Kendall Cornish and
Camryn La Sala
Updated Feb. 26, 2026, 3:23 p.m. ET
New York Post may receive revenue from affiliate and advertising partnerships for sharing this content and/or when you make a purchase.

Embarking on a journey, whether it’s across the globe or just a weekend getaway, begins with the best luggage brand by your side. With a myriad of options flooding the market, finding the perfect travel companion can be a daunting task.

Don’t worry — we explored a curated selection of the best luggage brands that combine durability, style, and functionality.

From sleek carry-ons to spacious suitcases and duffle bags, these brands have mastered the art of crafting luggage that not only withstands the rigors of travel but also makes a statement wherever you go, regardless of whether it’s for a romantic getaway or weekend trip.

There are tons to consider: design, warranty, overall quality (zippers, wheels, handles) and then, of course, optimal interior space that meets both your needs and airline requirements. We considered it all and bring you our list of the best luggage brands for carry-ons, checked bags and more.

Going on a plane for a *really* long time? Check out our guide to the best long flight essentials for more shopping.

Best Luggage Brand Overall: Monos

Pros

  • Sleek, modern design
  • Lightweight for easy maneuverability
  • Durable materials
  • Excellent customer service

Cons

  • Higher price point
  • Limited color options

Let us introduce you to Monos, the luggage brand that transformed our travel experience. With its sleek, minimalist design and premium materials, Monos luggage isn’t just functional — it’s a style statement. Every trip feels effortless with Monos; its lightweight construction and smooth-gliding wheels make navigating everything from airports to cobblestone streets a breeze. Plus, the attention to detail and durability ensure that our belongings stay safe and secure no matter where we go.

We tested the Check-In Large Suitcase ($375), which proved itself to be a workhorse, managing to fit an obscene amount of clothing without bulging awkwardly or feeling overstuffed. The shell flexed just enough to accommodate last-minute airport impulse purchases (hello, overpriced duty-free skincare) while still zipping up smoothly.

The wheels? Like butter. They glided effortlessly through polished terminals and over cracked sidewalks alike, proving their worth when sprinting through Heathrow with 10 minutes to spare. And while some luggage scuffs at the mere suggestion of a baggage carousel, Monos’ matte polycarbonate held up impressively well, showing only minor marks after several checked trips.

Monos was put through its paces in every imaginable scenario, from navigating JFK’s never-ending security lines to getting tossed (rather aggressively) into the trunk of a European rental car. My one small gripe? The color selection is more muted than some brands, but if you prefer timeless neutrals over neon “steal me” hues, it’s a non-issue.

The Post Wanted team also tested the Monos Backpack ($215) and continues to love its performance. “I immediately fell in love with the color and material, opting for the brown vegan leather,” Sophie Cannon, associate manager of commerce strategy and development, shared in her review. “The bag felt soft, like I like my leather to be, but the actual structure of the bag was stiff enough to stand up on it’s own while I packed. It also felt supportive and protective enough for my belongings, including a padded laptop sleeve and zipper pockets that felt secure.”

Warranty: Lifetime warranty (cosmetic flaws not accepted) | Return policy: Accepted within 100 days of receipt | TSA-approved lock: Yes |Shipping: Free within the contiguous U.S. |Color options: Vast; brights as well as dark and light neutrals

MONOS

Best Lightweight Luggage Brand: ROAM

Pros:

  • Complete personalization options
  • Smooth-rolling wheels
  • Durable construction

Cons:

  • Limited availability outside of online purchases

ROAM specializes in customizable hard-sided luggage, allowing travelers to personalize various components, including the front and back panels, zipper, lining, trim, and even add monograms. Their suitcases feature 360-degree spinning ball-bearing wheels, a locking telescoping handle, water-repellent zippers, and eco-friendly recycled linings. Each piece comes with a lifetime warranty and a 100-day satisfaction guarantee return policy.

We tried the ROAM Carry-On ($550) and found it to be a perfect blend of style and functionality. The customization process was seamless, resulting in a suitcase that truly reflected personal style. The suitcase’s lightweight yet sturdy construction made it easy to maneuver through airports, and the interior organization features kept belongings neatly in place during travel.

Customization is ROAM’s biggest flex, and the Carry-On delivers exactly what you’d expect from a made-to-order suitcase. The ability to choose colors for nearly every component meant I ended up with something uniquely mine (and never confused it with someone else’s bag again). Beyond the aesthetics, it’s a solid performer. The shell feels sturdy, the wheels roll smoothly, and the water-repellent zippers handled an unexpected downpour like a champ. The only downside? You can’t see it in person before purchasing, but if you love the idea of truly personalized luggage, it’s worth it.

Warranty: ROAM offers a lifetime warranty covering any manufacturing defects | Return Policy: Customers can return non-personalized items within 100 days of purchase for a full refund | TSA-Approved Lock: Yes | Shipping: Free standard shipping is available within the contiguous United States, with delivery times typically ranging from 5 to 7 business days | Color Options: Highly customizable with a wide range of color choices for various components

ROAM

Best Affordable Luggage Brand: Amazon

Pros

  • Affordable prices
  • Wide variety of options
  • Convenient for quick purchase and delivery
  • Access to customer reviews

Cons

  • Durability may vary
  • Basic design

Amazon’s luggage selection is like stepping into a traveler’s paradise. From affordable carry-ons to luxurious suitcases, they offer an extensive range of options to suit every budget and style. What we love most is the convenience of shopping from home and having our luggage delivered straight to our doorstep. With Amazon, we can browse through countless reviews and find the perfect travel companion without ever leaving our couch. Brands include Amazon Basics, Samsonite, American Tourister, and more.

We tested the Amazon Basics 20-Inch Hardside Spinner ($54) and found it to be durable and easy to maneuver. The orange hue was never difficult to spot in baggage claims, but it wasn’t as chic a carry-on as would be ideal. Be it because of the color or the finish, scuffs were minor and, on some trips, didn’t even seem to happen.

Amazon Basics isn’t going to win any style contests, but if you just need a suitcase that rolls, zips, and doesn’t fall apart after one trip, it’s a solid pick. The 20-inch Hardside Spinner was tested on everything from weekend city escapes to the dreaded red-eye home for the holidays.

The good: It’s lightweight, holds more than you’d expect, and survived multiple TSA beatdowns without losing a wheel. The bad: It’s not the smoothest roller, especially once packed to the brim, and the handle has a slight wobble that makes you question how long it will last. But at this price point, if it dies after a few years, replacing it isn’t painful.

Bonus points for the variety of colors — though, be warned, the bright ones scuff easily. If aesthetics matter, opt for a darker shade.

Warranty: Dependent on brand | Return policy: Accepted (no questions asked) | TSA-approved lock: Dependent on brand and style | Shipping: Prime members receive fast shipping (1-4 days) | Color options: Dependent on brand and style

AMAZON

Best Under-Seat: Lo & Sons

Pros

  • Compact size designed for easy storage
  • Functional features for organized packing
  • Versatile styles suitable for various occasions

Cons

  • Limited availability
  • May not suit all travelers’ needs

Lo & Sons isn’t just a luggage brand; it’s a lifestyle. Their meticulously designed bags blend fashion and function seamlessly, making them a favorite among frequent travelers like us. The clever organizational features, such as the detachable compartments and hidden pockets, have revolutionized the way we pack. What’s more, the timeless yet contemporary designs ensure that our Lo & Sons bag is always on-trend, no matter where we go. With Lo & Sons, we’re not just traveling; we’re making a statement.

We tried the O.G. 2 ($378, $189) and found it to be yet another go-to for every trip because of its suitcase handlebar sleeve, comfortable straps, elegant appearance, ability to be stuffed to the brim, secret shoe compartment, and outer laptop sleeve. It’s got it all and is made with materials that make it last.

Warranty: Dependant on faulty manufacturing or workmanship | Return policy: Unused products accepted within 30 days of receipt | TSA-approved lock: Dependant on product | Shipping: Free on purchases over $250; $9.95 for purchases above $60; $7.95 for purchases below $60 ; 3-10 business days | Color options: Black, navy, and dark neutrals

LO & SONS

Most Durable Luggage Brand: TravelPro

Pros

  • Lightweight construction
  • Ample storage compartments
  • Trusted brand among frequent travelers
  • Built to last

Cons

  • Limited style options

As seasoned travelers, we rely on TravelPro for its unmatched durability and clever organizational features. Its ample storage compartments and rugged construction ensure that our belongings stay secure, even during the most demanding journeys. With TravelPro, packing becomes a joy rather than a hassle, allowing us to embark on adventures with confidence and ease.

We tried the TravelPro x Travel + Leisure Compact Carry-On Spinner ($447) on seven international trips, from rain-soaked layovers in Amsterdam to taxi rides in Mexico, and it still looks shockingly new. The high-density ballistic nylon exterior makes it nearly impervious to scuffs, spills, and rough handling—whereas polycarbonate cases, even expensive ones, can crack or dent.

The telescoping handle is one of the best I’ve ever used. It’s solid, sturdy, and never wobbles. (Seriously, wobbly handles are my personal hell, and this one doesn’t budge.) The wheels are smooth but built for real-world terrain, meaning they roll easily on uneven sidewalks, hotel lobbies, and airport floors without that annoying vibration you get with cheaper luggage. Storage-wise, TravelPro nails the functional extras that make life easier. Expandable zippers let you cram in an extra outfit (or five), removable pouches and dividers keep things organized, and external compartments let you grab essentials mid-transit without rummaging through your entire bag. The layout makes packing simple rather than an Olympic sport.

Would I ever choose TravelPro over something more aesthetically pleasing? For sheer reliability, absolutely. It’s not a fashion statement like Rimowa or Béis, but it is a bulletproof investment for people who travel often and want a no-fuss, no-failure suitcase.

Warranty: Limited lifetime warranty | Return policy: Accepted within 100 days of purchase (no questions asked) | TSA-approved lock: Yes | Shipping: Free; 1-3 business days | Color options: Dependant on style; mostly dark neutrals

AMAZON
TRAVELPRO

Best Designed: Rimowa

Pros

  • Premium quality materials and craftsmanship
  • Luxury design
  • Exceptional durability
  • Prestige and status symbol

Cons

  • Highest price point
  • Limited accessibility for budget-conscious travelers

Luxury meets functionality with Rimowa, the luggage brand favored by discerning travelers worldwide. From its iconic grooved design to its lightweight yet durable construction, Rimowa luggage exudes sophistication in every detail. Traveling with Rimowa isn’t just about reaching your destination; it’s about embarking on a journey in style. With its unmatched craftsmanship and timeless elegance, Rimowa luggage has become an essential part of our travel repertoire.

We tried the Essential Sleeve Cabin Carry-On ($1,100) and consistently find it to be my absolute go-to for how smoothly it rolls, how easy it is to lift, how convenient the removable interior dividers are, how chic its appearance is, and how helpful the outside sleeve is (which all but replaced our need for another personal item). And we swear it’s what got us into a lounge once without having to pay a fee…lucky charm or status symbol?

Is Rimowa worth the price? In my humble opinion, absolutely — if you travel very frequently and value luggage that looks and performs flawlessly for years. It’s a steep price tag, but Rimowa’s lifetime service guarantee, high resale value, and pure longevity make it a better investment than constantly replacing mid-tier suitcases. Plus, let’s be honest, it just looks sexy.

Would I ever check a Rimowa? Only if my life depended on it. Rimowa’s cabin bags are meant to stay by your side, not risk being thrown into the abyss of a baggage hold. (The aluminum cases do hold up incredibly well to being checked, but I still wince every time I see one get loaded onto a conveyor belt.)

Warranty: Lifetime guarantee; dependent on wear and tear | Return policy: Unused products accepted within 30 days of delivery | TSA-approved lock: Yes | Shipping: Free on purchases over $300; 3-5 business days | Color options: Silver and black; limited edition colors available seasonally

RIMOWA

Best Sustainable: Paravel

Pros:

  • Eco-friendly materials
  • Thoughtful organizational features
  • Carbon-neutral production

Cons:

  • Limited color options
  • Potential for scuffs and scratches

Paravel focuses on sustainable and stylish luggage, utilizing recycled materials such as polycarbonate, aluminum, vegan leather, and plastic water bottles for their products. The Aviator Plus carry-on, for instance, is designed to fit 4–7 days of clothing and 2–4 pairs of shoes, featuring a TSA-compliant lock, compression board, separate shoe storage compartment, and an included laundry bag. Paravel’s Aviator luggage line is carbon neutral from production to shipping, including extra offsets for the first flight taken with it.

We tried the *Aviator Plus Carry-On ($425) and found it to be a totally reliable, great-looking, and even eco-friendly travel companion. The suitcase’s sturdy construction and smooth spinner wheels made navigating through airports and city streets effortless. The interior organization, including the compression board and separate shoe compartment, maximized packing efficiency. However, the light-colored exterior was prone to scuffs, which required some effort to clean.

Paravel proves that sustainability doesn’t have to mean sacrificing style. The recycled materials felt just as sturdy as traditional polycarbonate, and the carbon-neutral production added a feel-good factor to every trip. Packing efficiency was a major highlight: the compression board and separate shoe compartment kept things tidy, and the structured design helped avoid overpacking disasters.

One small issue? Lighter colors scuffed faster than expected, but nothing a quick wipe-down couldn’t fix. For anyone looking to reduce their carbon footprint while still traveling in style, it’s an easy recommendation.

Warranty: Paravel offers a 10-year warranty covering defects in materials and workmanship | Return Policy: Items can be returned within 30 days of receipt, provided they are unused and in original condition | TSA-Approved Lock: Yes | Shipping: Free standard shipping is available within the contiguous United States, with delivery times typically ranging from 5 to 7 business days | Color Options: Available in multiple color combinations, with options for monogramming

*Editor’s note: Paravel’s Aviator Plus Carry-On is currently out of stock. For a similar carry-on, we recommend Quince’s Carry-On Suitcase ($130), which has 360-degree spinning wheels, a removable laundry bag, a TSA-approved lock, and a telescope handle.

AMAZON

Best Organization: Solgaard

Pros

  • Innovative organization features
  • Durable materials
  • Sustainable practices

Cons

  • Higher price point
  • Limited availability

Solgaard offers the crème de la crème of luggage options and this one is our current favorite. The Carry-on Closet Suitcase can easily fit essentials, as well as those “just in case” items and is as durable as they come. Its exterior has an unbreakable polycarbonate shell, which keeps it in pristine condition in baggage claim. The interior’s patented built-in shelving system stores clothes perfectly — no stuffing or squeezing necessary. The suitcase also features an integrated USB charging port, a three-digit lock, and frictionless wheels for quiet transport. Available in nine different colors.

We were blown away by the thoughtfulness behind features like the built-in shelving system and the removable battery pack for charging devices on the go. With Solgaard, we not only travel with purpose but also with a sense of pride knowing that our luggage contributes to a greener planet.

We tried the Original Carry-On Closet ($275) and found it to be extremely helpful in the way of organization and its built-in USB charging port. It was also unique enough to be recognized easily in baggage claims and stylish enough to make its serious functionality seem like a bonus.

“Solgaard’s Carry On Closet is by far the most unique suitcase I’ve ever used,” Angela Tricarico, Decider commerce writer/reporter, shared after testing. “It’s perfect for weekend trips, but savvy packers could probably make the case work for longer trips than that, too. It’s all thanks to the Carry On Closet insert that you pack outside of the suitcase, then compress and insert into one side of the open case.”

The best part? Once you reach your destination, you can take it out and unpack in seconds, since everything is already folded neatly into a hanging organizer.

Warranty: Lifetime with registration | Return Policy: Accepted within 30 days of receipt | TSA-approved lock: Yes | Shipping: Free for orders over $100; 7-10 days | Color options: 9+ colorways; dependant on style

SOLGAARD

Best Quality Luggage Brand: Samsonite

Pros:

  • Brand offers a variety of luggage styles, including bags, backpacks, and hard shells
  • Known for stylish and durable designs

Cons:

  • Some reviews mention luggage dimensions not being exact

Samsonite is a globally renowned luggage brand known for its innovative design, superior quality, and durability. With over a century of experience, Samsonite consistently delivers stylish and functional travel solutions, making it a trusted choice for travelers worldwide. Its commitment to providing ample packing space and robust construction makes Samsonite the best choice for those looking for something highly reliable.

We tried the Samsonite Freeform Hardside Expandable suitcases ($299). In fact, Jacqueline Goldstein, The Post’s VP of Commerce, “registered for the [them] in the carry-on size when I got married, so my husband and I both have one: mine is white, his black. I am obsessed with it and how easy it is to move around because of the wheels that can move in all directions and the lightweight feel of gliding it. I have maneuvered through crowded areas like Bangkok or Barcelona’s Gothic Quarter with ease.”

We’ve done a lot of traveling over the past few years with these bags. I’ve learned to travel light, and I can do 2+ weeks, just packing for myself, in a carry-on and personal item. Over time, the bag has some light scuff marks, but it’s still in great shape and they don’t bother me. If you’re someone who wants your luggage pristine, keep this in mind and maybe consider a different material. For me, as long as my stuff is safe, I am happy.”

Best Carry-On Luggage: Away

Pros

  • Stylish designs available in various colors
  • Durable construction
  • Built-in battery charger for electronics

Cons

  • Higher price point
  • Limited selection
  • Popularity make it hard to locate in baggage claim

Away luggage is not just about the sleek design and fashionable colors — it’s about functionality and convenience. Whether it’s the built-in USB charging ports or the thoughtfully designed interior compartments, every detail of Away luggage is crafted with the modern traveler in mind. With Away, we feel confident knowing that our luggage can keep up with our jet-setting as well as our personal style.

We tried the small Carry-On ($248, $275) and found it to be attractive, easy to roll and lift, and we loved the ability to personalize it with our initials, which made it a cinch to find in the baggage claims (otherwise, it might be hard because of how ubiquitous Away suitcases are these days). Here’s our more in-depth review of Away luggage, too.

“For long weekends, I reach for The Bigger Carry-On Flex. When I’m checking a bag but still want to travel light, The Medium gives me the extra space without the bulk,” says Senior Commerce Editor, Camryn La Sala. “The Large is my go-to for extended getaways, big moves, or trips where I’ll be planted in one or two places — like a tropical escape to Hawaii with stops on a couple of islands. But if I’m hopping between cities every few days, especially in Europe with cobblestones galore, I’ll stick with The Medium to avoid lugging too much around.”

Away has become so ubiquitous that spotting one in an airport is like playing luggage bingo. The Carry-On was tested on both quick domestic flights and long-haul international trips, and it delivered in all the expected ways. It rolls well, fits into overhead bins (unless you’re on a regional jet, in which case, good luck), and has just the right amount of stretch to accommodate last-minute packing chaos. The optional built-in battery was handy for charging my phone on the go, though some airlines made me remove it before boarding.

It’s stylish, practical, and a solid investment. The only downside? Its popularity — expect to see identical bags all around baggage claim.

Warranty: Lifetime warranty | Return policy: Unused products accepted within 100 days of receipt | TSA-approved lock: Yes | Shipping: Free; 2-4 business days |Color options: Vast; includes brights and dark and light neutrals

AWAY

Best Luxury Luggage Brand: Royce & Rocket

Pros:

  • Extremely stylish
  • Highly organized with patented shelving technology
  • Reduces wrinkling of clothes
  • Silent wheels that rotate 360 degrees on all surfaces

Cons:

  • Some customers prefer not having built-in drawers

Royce & Rocket is the epitome of high-end luxury without spending thousands, thanks to the fine details woven into every product. From its TSA-approved luggage locks to the innovative fold-out drawers that keep your belongings organized, every element is designed with precision. This isn’t just a bag, it’s a practical indulgence you need in your life.

We tried the Royce & Rocket Castle Carry-on Expandable ($530). As someone who has been loyal to AWAY luggage for years, I was skeptical when I first encountered Royce & Rocket. But after giving their Castle carry-on a spin, I’m officially converted. AWAY felt like a dependable choice, Royce & Rocket delivers the Rolls Royce of travel bags. There is a lot of detail placed into this suitcase, from the regal embossed logo emblem on the front of the suitcase to the patented fold-out shelves that can organize your clothing and unmentionables. 

The reason I fell in love with this bag is simple: it eliminates the inconvenience of unpacking and repacking every time I check into a new hotel. I don’t have to unpack all of my belongings into drawers, just to do it all over again the next night at my next stop. The Royce & Rocket Castle carry-on has thoughtful organizers and pockets in its lining that help keep everything neatly sorted so that I can live out of my suitcase without the disorganized mess. 

Warranty: Lifetime guarantee | Return policy: Unused products accepted within 15 days of delivery | TSA-approved lock: Yes | Shipping: Free on purchases over $100. | Color options: 6 shades available.

ROYCE & ROCKET

Best Style: Béis Travel

Pros

  • Trendy designs with stylish aesthetics
  • Thoughtful features for convenience
  • Affordably priced for the quality

Cons

  • Limited variety
  • May not suit all tastes

Béis Travel, which was launched by actress Shay Mitchell in 2018, features a variety of trendy but chic luggage pieces for many different occasions and purposes. The brand offers more than just luggage; it’s a lifestyle choice for the fashion-conscious traveler. From trendy designs to thoughtful features, they’ve mastered the art of combining style and functionality. Whether it’s chic carry-ons or versatile accessories, Béis Travel has us covered. With Béis, we travel in style without breaking the bank, making every trip a fashion statement.

We tried the Medium Check-in Roller ($338) on multiple long-haul flights, and it delivered on all fronts. First impression? It looks luxe without trying too hard. The matte finish, neutral color palette, and minimalist design make it a suitcase you actually want to be seen with. But unlike some “trendy” luggage brands that focus on aesthetics over performance, Béis performs as well as it looks.

Let’s talk organization because Béis goes above and beyond here. The removable pouches, dividers, and built-in laundry bags make packing ridiculously efficient. Everything has a place, which means no more frantic, pre-flight digging for your passport or headphones. The expansion zipper also gives you extra room when needed because, let’s be real, no one ever packs less on the way home.

The wheels are super smooth (think Monos or Away level) but what actually shocked me? The waterproof exterior. During a particularly miserable, rainy airport transfer, my bag sat on the tarmac getting drenched, yet everything inside stayed completely dry. (A miracle, considering most fabric or even polycarbonate cases absorb water like a sponge.)

If I had one minor complaint, it would be its ubiquity. Béis is everywhere now, which means spotting your bag in baggage claim might take an extra minute. But for the price, it’s one of the best-looking, best-performing suitcases out there.

Additionally, The Weekender ($108) is another fan-favorite option at Post Wanted and one we reviewed as well. “TSA with my weekender was a breeze, as I easily found my laptop in the padded pocket and removed my baggy of liquids from the main inside compartment,” Cannon noted in her review. “The bag did just barely fit in the provided bins at the airport, but the bag does have little feet on the bottom and can ride on the conveyor belt alone, pending your airport’s rules.”

Warranty: Lifetime warranty | Return policy: Like-new products can be returned within 45 days of receipt | TSA-approved lock: Yes | Shipping: $14.95 for orders under $150; free for over $150; 1-3 business days | Color options: Depends on style; mostly light neutrals and bright limited edition hues

BéIS TRAVEL

Best Luggage Sets: July

Pros

  • Versatile sets
  • Innovative design features
  • 100-day trial period
  • Lifetime warranty

Cons

  • Higher price point
  • Limited availability outside of online purchase

Born in Melbourne, Australia — July luggage is beautiful and durable. Many of their suitcases feature a unique eggshell design to protect your goods, anodized aluminum corner bumpers to protect from hard drops, an integrated TSA lock so your contents are always secure and much more.

July’s modular design and premium craftsmanship have transformed the way we approach packing and organization. The customizable sets and intuitive features, like removable laundry bags and compression straps, adapt to our changing travel needs effortlessly. With July, we enjoy the flexibility to create our ideal travel companion, tailored to suit every destination and adventure, making each journey a personalized experience.

We tried the Carry-On & Checked Set ($640) on a mix of long-haul and domestic flights, and the first thing that stood out was the eggshell design — a smooth yet ultra-durable polycarbonate that resists scratches better than most hard-shell suitcases. (I’ve tested enough luggage to know that matte finishes show everything, but July’s cases somehow hold up better than expected.)

The corner bumpers are a genius addition and something that should be standard in all high-end luggage. Unlike cases that show wear on the edges first, July’s reinforced corners take the impact first, meaning fewer ugly dings over time.

Packing is where July shines. The interior is thoughtfully designed, with compression straps that actually work (some brands include them as a formality, but these actually keep things in place) and hidden laundry bags that are chef’s kiss when dealing with longer trips. The carry-on nests inside the checked bag for easy storage — a small but smart touch.

Warranty: Covers manufacturing faults on July products for either repair or replacement | Return policy: Like-new products can be returned for a refund 100 days after purchase | TSA-approved lock: Yes |Shipping: $15 if purchase under $100; free if over $100; 4-6 business days | Color options: 9 colors; 1 limited edition

JULY

Best for International Travel: Tumi

Pros

  • Premium quality
  • Excellent durability
  • Innovative features for travelers

Cons

  • Higher price point
  • Limited styles in some collections

Tumi, a luggage brand founded in 1975 by Charlie Clifford after a stint in the Peace Corps in Peru, has been a cult favorite for decades. The brand offers a variety of suitcase sizes ranging from weekender bags (with wheels and without) to mega-large duffle bags. Plus, they have a warranty of five years on most of their products.

Tumi’s reputation for premium quality and innovation has made it our top choice for luxury travel essentials. The exceptional durability and advanced features, such as TSA-approved locks and impact-resistant materials, offer unparalleled peace of mind on every trip. With Tumi by our side, we travel with confidence, knowing that we’re equipped with the best-in-class luggage that’s as stylish as it is functional.

How We Tested

To ensure a fair and thorough evaluation, each piece of luggage underwent at least two trips over the past six years, ranging from weekend getaways to long-haul international flights, to gauge real-world performance. Our testing process prioritized the following key factors:

Wear and tear: Durability under real-world conditions

A suitcase may look great initially, but long-term durability is crucial. We tested for:

  • Scuff resistance: We assessed how well different materials (polycarbonate, aluminum, nylon, and textile) resisted scratches, dents, and stains after repeated handling, including being placed in overhead bins, checked into cargo holds, and dragged over rough terrain.
  • Impact protection: Drop tests were conducted to evaluate how well the luggage protected fragile items when fully packed. Hard-shell suitcases were checked for cracks and flexing, while soft-sided models were tested for abrasion and water resistance.
  • Handles and zippers: Telescoping handles were stress-tested for wobble and durability when extended and retracted frequently. Zippers were evaluated for smoothness, resistance to snagging, and water resistance in wet conditions.

User experience: Ease of travel and special features

Luggage should be an asset, not a hassle, during travel. We tested how easily each piece handled different environments, including airport terminals, train stations, cobblestone streets, and stair-heavy locations.

  • Rolling and carrying: Spinner wheels were assessed for smoothness, stability, and performance on uneven surfaces. Rolling resistance was measured when bags were full versus empty. Handles, both retractable and side/top grips, were tested for comfort and ergonomics.
  • Weight and packing features: We evaluated the empty weight of each bag relative to its storage capacity, ensuring a good balance of lightweight materials and durability. Compression features, interior organization, and expandability were also tested to determine packing efficiency.
  • Storage and expandability: Suitcases were overpacked to see how well they handled stretching and compression. Expandable zippers and integrated compression panels were evaluated for functionality and durability over repeated use.

Style: Aesthetic appeal & customization

For those who value travel aesthetics, we assessed how stylish, versatile, and distinctive each brand’s designs were.

  • Visual appeal: We considered whether each piece had a timeless, modern, or trendy look and whether the materials aged well over time.
  • Customization: For brands that offer personalization, we explored the level of customization available, from color choices to monogramming.
  • Material finish: Matte, glossy, and textured finishes were tested to see how they held up against scratches, dirt, and smudging.

Longevity

A well-made suitcase should last for years, so we examined the durability of materials, construction quality, and brand warranty policies.

  • Structural integrity: After multiple trips, we checked for handle wobbling, wheel misalignment, broken zippers, and exterior wear.
  • Material quality: Soft-shell cases were evaluated for fraying, while hard-shell models were inspected for cracks, warping, or fading.
  • Warranty and repairs: We researched each brand’s repair and warranty policies, noting how comprehensive their coverage was and how easy it was to get replacements or repairs.

This rigorous testing process allowed us to identify the standout brands in each category, ensuring that every recommendation meets high standards for durability, usability, and overall travel performance.

FAQs

Which brand has the best luggage?

Monos is widely regarded as one of the best luggage brands due to its combination of quality, design, and functionality. Their luggage is known for its sleek, minimalist aesthetic and robust construction, often featuring high-grade polycarbonate shells that offer both durability and lightweight performance.

Additionally, Monos places a strong emphasis on customer experience, providing a lifetime warranty and exceptional customer service. The thoughtful interior design, with compartments and pockets for easy organization, further enhances its appeal. Overall, Monos delivers a premium travel experience that justifies its reputation as a top luggage brand.

What type of luggage is most durable?

Hardshell luggage, particularly those made from materials like polycarbonate, aluminum, or ABS, is generally considered the most durable type of luggage. Polycarbonate offers a great balance between durability and weight, making it resistant to impact and capable of withstanding the rigors of travel.

Aluminum luggage, while heavier, provides exceptional strength and durability, often featuring reinforced corners and a sturdy frame that can endure rough handling. These materials protect against scratches, dents, and other forms of damage, ensuring that the luggage maintains its structural integrity and appearance over time.

Which is better, luggage soft or hard?

The choice between soft and hard luggage depends on the traveler’s needs and preferences. Hard luggage, made from materials like polycarbonate or aluminum, offers superior protection for fragile items and is more resistant to impacts and weather conditions. It is also easier to clean and often features more secure locking mechanisms.

On the other hand, soft luggage, typically made from durable fabrics like ballistic nylon or polyester, is more flexible, allowing for easier fitting into tight spaces and offering external pockets for additional storage. Soft luggage is often lighter and can expand to accommodate more items. Therefore, if protection and security are paramount, hard luggage is better; if flexibility and convenience are more important to you, soft luggage may be the preferable choice.
